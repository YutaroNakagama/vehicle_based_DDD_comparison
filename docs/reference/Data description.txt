Data description:
	-Within each subject’s folder there are two matlab data files for each data stream.   Each subject performs two different experimental conditions.  For example,  <data stream name> _1.mat is the first portion of the  experiment and <data stream name>_2.mat is the second portion of the experiment.  
    -Each data stream has a relative time stamp to all other data streams.  All the time stamps are referenced to the start and stop of each experiment, meaning time 0 is when that experiment started. 


The data streams are:

AudioEvents, n by 9 cell array.   Where n is the number of audio events that occured during the trial.  There are usually 20 audio events during a trial, although there are instances when there are less than 20. The 20 audio events typically consist of 15 questions which are triggered by the distance traveled and 5 questions that are timed to the braking event (when the car in front of the subject's car brakes in speed and the brake lights are illuminated.).  
   1st colunm =  Subject number 
   2nd colunm = trial  (1 or 2)
   3rd column = Audio track identifier.  (1-40; Numbers 31-40 are the SOA audio events. Numbers 1-15 are used in the 1st trial and 16-30 are used in the 2nd trial.
   4th column = text of the audio
   5th column = start of audio, time in seconds since begininng of trial.
   6th column = end of audio,  time in seconds since begininng of trial. 
   7th column = SOA time.  Usually not applicable (nan), otherwise it is the time, in seconds, between the end of the question and the illumination of the brake lights of the car in front of the subject.  
   8th column = FTO,  delay, in seconds, between the end of the question and the subject's answer.  A reaction time measure.
   9th column = duration of subject's answer, in seconds.  


-*.m4a files (Audio).  The audio file’s two channels are made up of the lapel microphone and the audio channel that the subject hears from the headphones during the experiment.  The channels have been amplified and the data points have been synchronized between the 2 channels.

-fNIRS,   This is the raw fnirs’ output.  NIRSdata is a 42xn array, where n is the number of samples.  The first row is the LSL time stamp.  The second is the frame number. The next 40 rows are the frnirs data.  The timestamps are in seconds and is directly related to all the other data streams’ time stamps.
          NIRSinfo is made up of 3 structure fields; *.SRCinds which is and array of the SOURCE indicies. *.DETinds is the detecter indicies.  *.LAMDA is the wavelengths and *.DIS is the distance.

-Physio,  This is a 5xn array, where is n is the number of samples.  
	1st row = time stamps, in seconds
    2nd row = Blood pressure, in mmHg
	3rd row = skin conductance, in muS
	4th row = respiration, in z scores (x – xmean)/std x
	5th row = O2, in percentage
	

-PupilData,  this contains 2 arrays, each 3xn (n = # of samples)
	Pupil diameter 2D model  first row = timestamp, left eye pupil diameter, right eye pupil diameter 
	Pupil diameter 3D model  first row = timestamp, left eye pupil diameter, right eye pupil diameter 

-SIMlsl (Post SOA change, eg sub num from 100 to 213), This contains a 31xn array, n is the number of samples.
  1st row LSL time
  2nd is Sim time (from the lsl data file)
  3rd is Sim time (from the Sim data file-not the lsl file)
  4 : Brake_Angle	deg    [24]
  5 : Brake_Event_Time	s      [25]
  6 : Audio_File_Num	(ND)   [27]
  7 : Audio_Time	s      [29]
  8 : JS_time	s              [30]
  9 : DRT_Onset	1=light on     [32]
  10: DRT_Resp	1=button press [33]
  11: DRT_Button_Value	v      [34]
  12: SOA	(ND)           [35]
  13: DRT_Onset_Time	s      [36]
  14: DRT_Resp_Time	s      [37]
  15: DRT_Num	(ND)           [38]
  16: Brake_Lights	1=on   [39]
  17: Eval	1=keep         [40]
  18: Route_Position	m      [41]
  19: Long_Acceleration	m/s/s  [42]
  20: Lat_Acceleration	m/s/s  [43]
  21: Throttle_Position	deg    [44]
  22: Brake_Force	N      [45]
  23: Gear	(ND)           [46]
  24: Heading_Error	deg    [48]
  25: Headway_Distance	m      [49]
  26: Headway_Time	s      [50]
  27: Lane_Num	(ND)           [51]

  31: Velocity	m/s            [57]  

     
-EEG
  EEG data, bandpass filtered from 0.25Hz to 125Hz (no phase shift). The data is also common mode averaged, meaning each EEG channel is referenced against the average of all the channels (helps attenuate movement artifacts).  
  
  Data is in a 9xn matrix, where n is the number of samples. 
  
  1st row:  LSL time stamps, in seconds
  2nd row:  Channel FC5, nV
	3rd row:  Channel FC1, nV
	4th row:  Channel CP5, nV
	5th row:  Channel CP1, nV
	6th row:  Channel CP2, nV
	7th row:  Channel CP6, nV
	8th row:  Channel FC2, nV
	9th row:  Channel FC6, nV

-EventTimes
  Each trial has 10 events. Of these 10 events 4 of them are fake events, where the car is positioned in front of the driver but does not break.
  During these event windows questions are asked and the participant has to answer.
 
  Contains the timestamps of events of interest:
    DRT_response: 1xn vector of the subjects responses, in seconds in LSL time, to the DRT signal.
    DRT_SignalOn: 1xn vector of timestamps (seconds LSL time) when the DRT signal is active.
    DRT_rt: 1xn vector of the reaction time (seconds) from a DRT_SignalOn to the subjects response (DRT_response).  Some elements of DRT_rt will be nan.  This is a signal that did not get a response.    
    BrakeLights_On: 1xn vector of timestamps when the forward car's brake lights turn on
    BrakeReactionTime: 1xn reaction time in seconds of when the brake angle changed from the time the brake light turned on.
    EventTime: 10x2 array of the start and stop time of an event time window.  The first number in each row is the start time of the event window.  The second is the end.
    EventDiscription: 10x2 array.  Each row is an event.  The first column is 1 = braking 0 = fake;  The second column is 1 = DRT, 0 = no DRT.  The second column will either be all ones or all zeros, depending on if that trial was a DRT trial or noDRT trial. 


-BehaviorMatrix
	There are 10 files for each subject's trial.  The file names BehviorMatrix_S0<SubjectNumber>_<TrialNumber>_<BinningWindowSizeInSeconds>.mat   The BinningWindowSizeInSeconds ranges from 1 through 10 seconds. This obviously determines the size of the behavior matrix array.  For instance,  BehaviorMatrix_S0101_1_1.mat is a 1450x13 logical array, where as, BehaviorMatrix_S0101_1_10.mat  is a 145x13 logical array.
	As for the 13 logical markers (per time window),....  The logical marker denotes an event that happened before the time window and after the time window -1.  That means that the time window bins count one time window before that time window.  For example if the missed DRT is recorded at 13.1098 then it will register in the 14th time window bin, confusing I know but its all the same in the end,.....  The 13 logical markers in the array are:

1).  This denotes a missed DRT signal within that time window.  For instance, BehaviorMatrix_S0103_1_1.mat has 6 missed DRT signals (sum(Data(:,1)) and they happened at the time windows of (find(Data(:,1)): 484 494 542 832 1059 1149.

2).  Slow DRT responses.  This notes the slowest DRT reaction times for this subject.  The threshold is defined by the lower than the 25th percentile of the reaction times, or 1st quartile.

3).  Fast DRT responses.  The fastest reaction times to the DRT signal.  The threshold is defined by the 75th percentile or greater, or 3rd quartile.

4).  Slow Communication responses.  If the subjects response was below the slowest 25th percentile then the time the questions starts through the answers end is denoted.  In other words,  if there is a lot of time between the end of the question and the start of the subjects answer (i.e., the subjects reaction time to the question) then the whole communication event is marked (start of question - end of subjects answer). 

5).  Fast Communication responses.  Subjects response was above the 75th percentile of reaction time of the subject to the question. 

6).  Slow Braking reactions.  Takes the subject's slowest reaction time to the response of the brake lights from the car in front of the subjects.  This includes the whole braking event which is when the forward car appears and brakes and then accelerates away. The whole event is ~ 30 secs   

7).  Fast Braking reactions.  Takes the subject's fastest reaction time to the response of the brake lights from the car in front of the subjects.   This includes the whole braking event which is when the forward car appears and brakes and then accelerates away. The whole event is ~ 30 secs   


Steering:  The fast and slow measures are taken from the absolute value of the change in steering wheel angle (in degrees)  So,  slow steering is analogous to a controlled steady movement of the steering wheel.  Whereas fast is going to be rapid changes. 

8).  Slow Steering.  Takes the bottom 15 percent of the change in the steering wheel angle.   

9).  Fast Steering.  Takes the top 85 percent of the change in the steering wheel angle.


Position error is calculated by taking the mean absolute distance per time window from the center line of the driving lane.  

10).  Position Offset-low -  the smallest amount of error for that subject (to the 15th percentile).

11).  Position Offset-hi	-  the largest amount of error for that subject (from the 85th percentile). 

error is calculated by taking the mean absolute distance per time window from the zeroth degree (straight heading). 

12).  heading offset - low   the lowest 15% (below the 15th percentile)  of the subject's heading error.

13).  heading offset - hi   the highest 15% (above the 85th percentile) of the subject's heading error.
